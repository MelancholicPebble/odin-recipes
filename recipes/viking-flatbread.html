Viking Flatbread

Adapted from The Viking Warrior Company

Bread, regardless of the exact location, was, and always will be a staple of any European cuisine. This fact of life was true for the Vikings, just as it was true for their continental contemporaries.

That being said, their nomadic lifestyles paired with the harsh tundra climate meant some adjustments had to be made. The following is a flatbread recipe which compliments just about any other recipe on the website.

First, prepare the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups of whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 tps of salt

The Vikings seldom had access to any type of oven, which means you won't be needing one either! Simply follow these steps:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt
  2. Once thoroughly mixed, gradually begin adding the water while stirring. Soon, you'll have made a soft dough, which you should knead until it becomes smooth
  3. Divide the smooth dough into 6-8 pieces, and roll each one out into a thin, round flatbread
  4. Heat a dry frying pan over medium heat. Next, individually cook the flatbreads for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown
  5. Serve warm, perhaps alongisde another dish?
